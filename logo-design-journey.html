Design Rounds

Round 1

Initial Exploration

February 2026

Four initial concepts exploring different approaches: combination marks, lettermarks, minimalism, and emblems. Tested the project's color palette across gold and dark palettes with multiple layout formats (horizontal, vertical, icon-only).

1. Classic Elegance 2. Heritage Monogram 3. Danish Minimalism 4. Gentleman's Mark
Client Feedback “Feels too much like an old school computer game. We want vintage — the style they used back in the day when they would wear these type of hats.” Client referenced Lock & Co. Hatters as design inspiration.
View Round 1 Concepts
Round 2

Vintage Direction

February 2026

Five refined concepts embracing authentic vintage aesthetics — Victorian hatbox labels, 1920s Art Deco signage, Lock & Co.’s institutional restraint, wax seal trade marks, and a founder tribute design. Shifted to muted warm tones, hand-crafted detail, ornamental borders, and serif typography with character.

A. Hatter's Label B. Art Deco C. Refined Wordmark D. Gentleman's Seal E. Pepino's Legacy
Client Feedback Likes the old-school style where “people who know will know” — it tells a story. But must be immediately clear to anyone that it’s a hat shop. Carry forward: A (Victorian Label), D (Gentleman’s Seal), borrow readability from C.
View Round 2 Concepts
Round 3

Refinement — “Rewarding Depth, Instant Clarity”

February 2026

Six refined variations merging the best of Concepts A, C & D. Exploring the full spectrum from maximum heritage detail to maximum modern clarity, with each variation making a different trade-off. Heritage details for hat enthusiasts, instant readability for everyone.

1. Heritage Label Evolved 2. Seal Refined 3. Frame & Seal 4. Hat First 5. Clean Stamp 6. Signature
View Round 3 Variations
Final Selection

The Gentleman’s Seal — AI-Generated Illustration

February 2026

Combining the best of Round 2’s Concept D (Gentleman’s Seal) with the Frame & Seal refinement direction from Round 3. The final mark was generated using AI illustration (Gemini Pro) from detailed prompts capturing our Victorian wax-seal aesthetic — then paired with hand-set typography for the wordmark lockups.

Victorian Wax Seal Engraving Hatching Gold & Brown Palette Scalloped Edge
Design Principle “Rewarding depth, instant clarity” — A detailed engraved top hat in a scalloped wax seal. Hat enthusiasts see the craft; everyone sees a hat shop. The logo tells a story at close range while reading clearly at any size.

The Selected Mark

The Gentleman’s Seal — where Victorian craft meets Danish hat tradition

Heritage Hat Brands Our References

Lock & Co.
Hatters
Est. 1676
● London, England ● Royal Warrant Holder

The world's oldest hat shop. Invented the bowler hat in 1849. Their restrained, institutional identity — deep forest green, serif wordmark, generous whitespace — was our primary reference for Concept C (Refined Wordmark).

Website Heritage Page Brand Design by Deep
CHRISTY'S
Est. 1773
● London, England ● Royal Supplier since 1850s

Britain's foremost hat manufacturer since Prince Albert began wearing Christy's top hats. Their hatbox labels and trade cards from the 1890s–1920s directly inspired Concept A (Hatter's Label) and Concept D (Gentleman's Seal).

Website Heritage Page
Borsalino
Est. 1857
● Alessandria, Italy ● Grand Prix, Paris Exposition

Italian luxury fedoras — inseparable from Bogart in Casablanca. Their 1920s poster art by Marcello Dudovich and Leonetto Cappiello epitomize Art Deco hat advertising and inspired Concept B (Copenhagen Art Deco).

Website History Cappiello Posters
STETSON
Est. 1865
● Philadelphia, USA ● "Boss of the Plains"

Creator of the cowboy hat. By 1886, the world's largest hat company. Their iconic 1920s poster “The Last Drop from His Stetson” by Lon Megargee is still used in hat linings today. Referenced for American rugged heritage character.

Website About History (EU)
Herbert Johnson
Est. 1889
● London, England ● Indiana Jones' Hatter

Founded on the advice of the Prince of Wales. Made Indiana Jones' fedora, David Bowie's hat in The Man Who Fell to Earth, and the Joker's hat in Batman. Their Victorian-era craftsmanship and adventure spirit informed Concept E (Pepino's Legacy).

Website History
MÜHLBAUER
Est. 1903
● Vienna, Austria ● 4th Generation

Viennese minimalism meets traditional craft. Founded by Julianna Mühlbauer — a rare female entrepreneur in 1903. Their modern-clean approach with heritage roots shows how vintage craft can feel contemporary. General palette reference.

Website History
KANGOL
Est. 1938
● Cumbria, England ● silK + ANGora + woOL

From WWII military berets to Beatles licensing to hip-hop culture (LL Cool J, Notorious B.I.G.). Shows how a heritage brand can cross eras and audiences. Their bold branding with Union Jack tags represents a different approach — studied as contrast.

Website Our Story
Optimo Hats
Est. 1995
● Chicago, USA ● Museum-Quality Bespoke

Modern revival of Golden Age hatmaking. Graham Thompson apprenticed under legendary Chicago hatmaker Johnny Tyus, then spent decades recovering the lost art. Their workshop is a “working museum” of rare hatmaking tools. Shows that vintage craft can be a living tradition.

Website The Story
