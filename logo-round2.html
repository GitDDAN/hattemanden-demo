Design Direction: Round 2

Based on client feedback, Round 1 felt too digital and “computer game-like.” For Round 2 we moved toward authentic vintage aesthetics — the kind of typography, ornamentation, and warmth found on Victorian hatbox labels, 1920s Art Deco signage, and heritage brands like Lock & Co. of London.

Key shifts: muted warm tones (cognac, aged gold, taupe), hand-crafted line detail, ornamental borders, serif typography with character, and fine decorative rules. Each concept references a specific era or style tradition.

Reference: lockhatters.com — institutional serif authority, warm taupe-brown palette, restrained elegance

A. The Hatter's Label

Victorian Trade Label

Inspired by Victorian-era hatbox labels and haberdashery signage. Double-rule ornamental frame with corner accents, a detailed top hat illustration with fine engraving-style lines, and Playfair Display serif wordmark. Feels like opening a hatbox from a proper gentleman's outfitter.

Think: labels inside Christy's of London hat boxes, ca. 1890–1920

B. Copenhagen Art Deco

1920s Geometric

Geometric elegance from the golden age of hat-wearing. Stepped Art Deco corners, a geometric top hat rendered with strong horizontal lines (mimicking woodcut prints), and Cinzel typography — monumental and authoritative. Diamond-shaped decorative accents throughout.

Think: Art Deco hotel signage in Copenhagen, 1925–1935. The Peaky Blinders era.

C. The Refined Wordmark

Lock & Co. Inspired

Maximum restraint. Pure serif typography with minimal ornament — just the name, fine rules, and a subtle three-dot decorative element. Directly inspired by Lock & Co. Hatters of St. James's Street, London. The hat icon appears only in the vertical and icon variants. The warm taupe-brown (#88754a) color is borrowed from Lock's own palette.

Think: Lock & Co. of London (est. 1676), 350 years of understated authority

D. The Gentleman's Seal

Wax Seal / Trade Mark

A circular trade mark seal with curved text, decorative dot border, diamond separators, and a central hat illustration with fine engraving detail. Works as a standalone stamp, watermark, or brand seal. Adjacent Playfair Display wordmark in title case for a more approachable, personal feel. Particularly strong as a favicon and for packaging.

Think: wax seals on Victorian correspondence, trade mark stamps on quality goods

E. Pepino's Legacy

Founder Tribute

A vintage trade card style honouring founder Svend Erik “Pepino” Kjær. Features a detailed top hat illustration with engraving-style lines, a classic ribbon banner carrying the name, decorative flourishes, and the founder's attribution — “Grundlagt af Pepino”. The most story-driven concept, connecting the logo directly to the brand's 25-year heritage and family legacy.

Think: vintage trade cards, hatbox lid illustrations, Danish haberdashery ephemera
